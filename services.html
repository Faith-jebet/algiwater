Sand Filters

Sand filtration is used for the removal of suspended matter, as well as floating and sinkable particles. The waste water flows vertically through a fine bed of sand

and gravel part.The yield of a sand filter is determined by two sand filter functions: surface filtration and depth filtration. Surface filtration involves collecting the particles above the filter bed.These particles jointly form a macro-porous lump, which can collect new particles in a very effective manner. Depth filtration generally involves smaller particles that are more difficult to assemble and bound to the sand particles by adsorption. Particles are then removed by way of absorption or physical encapsulation.

Ultra-Filtration

Ultrafiltration (UF) is a process in which hydrostatic pressure forces a liquid against a semi-permeable membrane. Suspended solids and solutes of high

molecular weight are retained, while water and low molecular weight solutes pass through the membrane.
